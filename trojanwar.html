Trojan War

Achilles was the greatest warrior of all the Greek forces in Troy. His allegience, however, was not resolute. As detailed in Homer's Illiad, Achilles was a loose cannon whose actions had major consequences for both armies.

  1. Achilles' Arrival at Troy: Achilles joined the Greek army at Troy with his Myrmidon forces after accepting Odysseus's proposal.
  2. Dispute with Agamemnon: Achilles clashed with Greek commander Agamemnon and, feeling dishonored, withdrew from the battle.
  3. Patroclus' Death: While Achilles remained out of battle, his close friend Patroclus pretended to be him to rally the Greek armies. Patroclus fought bravely but was killed by Trojan pince Hector.
  4. Achilles Returns to Battle: Enraged by Patroclus' death, Achilles rejoined the war with the sole aim of killing Hector and avenging his friend.
  5. Achilles Kills Hector: Achilles and Hector fought in a legendary duel outside the gates of Troy. After slaying Hector, Achilles dishonored his body by dragging it behind his chariot.
  6. Achilles' Death: Later in the war, Achilles was killed by an arrow shot by Paris, Hector's brother. The arrow struck him in his only weakness.
